Pedestrians and Cyclists

What you can do

Be Visible

Be Safe and Be Seen

  • Wear bright/light colored clothing and reflective materials
  • Carry a flashlight when walking at night.
  • Cross the street in a well-lit area at night.
  • Stand clear of buses, hedges, parked cars, or other obstacles before crossing so drivers can see you.
Be Visible Be Safe and Be Seen

Be Alert

Avoid Dangerous Behaviors

  • Always walk on the sidewalk. If there is no sidewalk, walk facing traffic.
  • Don't assume vehicles will stop. Make eye contact with drivers, don't just look at the vehicle. If a driver is on a cell phone, he or she may not be paying enough attention to drive safely.
Be AlertAvoid Dangerous Behaviors

Cross Carefully

Look Before You Step

  • Cross streets at marked crosswalks or intersections, if possible.
  • Obey traffic signals such as WALK/DON'T WALK signs
  • Watch for turning vehicles. Make sure the driver sees you and will stop for you.
Cross Carefully Look Before You Step

Look left, right,

and left again

  • Look left, right, and left again before crossing a street.

Did you check?

Well, check again

  • Look across ALL lanes you must cross and visually clear each lane before proceeding. Even if one motorist stops, do not presume drivers in other lanes can see you and will stop for you
  • Watch for turning vehicles. Make sure the driver sees you and will stop for you.

Riding a bike?

Ride safely

  • Wear a properly fitted helmet, protect your brain and save your life
  • See and be seen! Embrace the bright colors, and lights
  • Watch and avoid road hazards, those potholes can getchya

Road Rules

Avoid the Rage

  • Go with the traffic flow
  • Yield to traffic when appropriate
  • Be predictable, nobody likes surprises
Road Rules Avoid the rage

Avoid Crashes

You're better alive

  • Bicyclist deaths occur most often between 6 p.m. and 9 p.m.
  • Alcohol was involved in 35 percent of all fatal bicyclist crashes in 2016. Ride responsibly.
  • Ride a bike that works—it really doesn’t matter how well you ride if the brakes don’t work.
